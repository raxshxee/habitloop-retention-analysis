# Final Project Pitch

## Project Name

HabitLoop Retention Intelligence Benchmark

## What It Is

A real-data competitive retention benchmark that analyzes 3,992 public App Store reviews across four consumer app categories:

- Language learning: Duolingo vs Babbel
- Meditation and sleep: Headspace vs Calm
- Self-care journaling: Finch vs Daylio
- Routine and habit building: Fabulous vs Habitica

The project converts customer review language into retention signals, lifecycle stages, risk levels, competitor patterns, and campaign recommendations.

## Why It Matters

Retention marketing roles are not only about writing emails. They require customer insight, segmentation, journey mapping, campaign triggers, testing mindset, and performance thinking. This project demonstrates those capabilities using public data.

The core idea:

Raw customer language becomes a lifecycle decision.

Examples:

- Pricing complaints become renewal/save-flow opportunities.
- Streak loss becomes engagement or reactivation strategy.
- Support and bug complaints become trust-repair flows.
- Positive habit value becomes advocacy or referral timing.

## What Makes It More Than Review Sorting

The project does not stop at labels. It builds a decision chain:

Review text -> retention signal -> lifecycle stage -> risk level -> competitor benchmark -> campaign flow -> segment -> success metric

That final chain is the project value. It shows how a retention marketer can move from messy voice-of-customer data to an actionable lifecycle plan.

## Best Interview Explanation

I wanted to avoid a simulated marketing dataset, so I used real public App Store reviews as a voice-of-customer source. I arranged the apps into competitive pairs so the project was not random: language learning, meditation, self-care journaling, and habit building. Then I classified review language into retention signals, mapped those signals to lifecycle stages, and built a dashboard that compares competitor pain points and recommends campaign flows with triggers, segments, and success metrics.
